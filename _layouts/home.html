I am a research scientist interested in individualizing brain stimulation and neuroimaging.
With this and growing as a research scientist, my research focuses on translational approaches between computational models, healthy controls and patients, specifically focusing on:

1) Common mechanisms across neuropsychiatric disorders (transnosographic approach)
2) Individualization of analyses

I am also deeply interested in open access science and contributing to research reproducibility.

With this in mind, I am currently looking for a Postdoctoral position.
Previously, I obtained a PhD in Neuroscience, with honors, from Lyon 1 University. My project was supervised by Dr MF Suaud-Chagny in the PSYR2 Team of the Lyon Neuroscience Research Center (France). The thesis work fit into a broad research axis intended to clarify the brain mechanisms underlying transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS) in healthy and clinical populations. More specifically, I investigated this question on a mechanistic level in relation to the impact of frontal tDCS montages on the dopaminergic system, combining tDCS with several imaging techniques (e.g., PET, resting-state fMRI, ASL, DTI).
